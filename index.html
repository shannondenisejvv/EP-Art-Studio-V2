The Artist

I began my artistic journey in 1975 with pottery.

I began my artistic journey in 1975 with pottery, exploring wheelwork, hand-building, and mould-making as forms of creative expression.

In 1998, I transitioned into painting—initially to decorate my ceramics—but quickly fell in love with the medium itself. I started with acrylics and later moved to oils, which remain my favourite.

With the guidance of inspiring teachers Dennis Hilton Lees and Cyril Coetzee, I developed both confidence and a love for experimentation that continues to shape my work.

The Story

The sea has always been my greatest source of inspiration.

I have been a member of the South African Society of Artists (SASA) since 2012.

The sea has always been my greatest source of inspiration. Growing up in Durban and now living along the shoreline of Saldanha, I am constantly drawn to coastal landscapes.

Plein air painting is a passion of mine, allowing me to capture fleeting light, shifting skies, and the ever-changing mood of the ocean.

Colour plays a central role in my work—I rarely use paint straight from the tube, preferring to mix my own tones to create depth and emotion.
